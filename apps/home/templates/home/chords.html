Introduction: Foundation

  • Chords are a simple way to start playing music on your guitar. They run A through G (with Majors and Minors and other variations) and are the most common way to get to jamming out and having a good time. Chords originate from scales and variations of scales, which are themselves a grouping notes that when played together complement each other in a certain way.
  • That said, chords are simply made of notes, which also run A-G, having sharp and flat versions. Sharp notes usually have a higher pitch of the same note (an uplifting sound) whereas Flat notes have a more dull or less enthusiastic sound. Each have their place in any song or chord/note progression.

Chords, Notes, Frets, Strings

  • Each string is tuned to an open note (standard tuning low-high: E A D G B E)
    • Other Common Tunings (low to high)
      • Standard Half Step Down (Flat): (Eb Ab Db Gb Bb Eb)
      • Drop D: (D A D G B E)
      • Drop D Half Step Down (Flat): (Db Ab Db Gb Bb Eb)
      • Drop C: (C G C F A D)
      • Drop C Half Step Up (Sharp): (C# G# C# F# A# D#)
  • Each note of each string represents a note between A and G
  • Chords represent groups of notes in the same scale that sound sharp or flat and together create a richer sound
  • Frets make it easy to keep track of which note you are playing (notice the dots on your fretboard and guitar neck)
  • C♯ -> indicates C Sharp, which is a half step up from C (as opposed to whole step: D)
  • C♭ -> indicates C Flat, which is a half step down from C (as opposed to whole step: B)
